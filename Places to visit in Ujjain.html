Places To Visit In Ujjain

Explore Ujjain

Ujjain is one of the holiest cities in India, situated on the eastern bank of the Kshipra River. It is one of the seven sacred cities (Sapta Puri) (Ayodhya, Mathura, Haridwar (Maya), Varanasi (Kashi), Kanchipuram (Kanchi), Ujjain (Avantika), and Dwarka (Dwaravati)) of the Hindus and is famous for its temples, ghats, and rich cultural heritage.

Here are some must-visit places in Ujjain that you can easily access from Rai Guest House.

Mahakaleshwar Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India, the Mahakaleshwar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its Bhasma Aarti, a special ritual performed early in the morning.

  • Distance from Rai Guest House: 2 km
  • Timings: 4:00 AM to 11:00 PM
  • Special Attraction: Bhasma Aarti at 4:00 AM

Kal Bhairav Temple

Dedicated to Kal Bhairav, a fierce manifestation of Lord Shiva, this temple is famous for the unique offering of liquor to the deity.

  • Distance from Rai Guest House: 3 km
  • Timings: 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM

Harsiddhi Temple

This temple is dedicated to Goddess Harsiddhi, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. The temple is known for its unique architecture and the two tall pillars adorned with lamps.

  • Distance from Rai Guest House: 2.5 km
  • Timings: 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM
  • Special Attraction: Evening aarti with illuminated pillars

Shipra River Ghats

The ghats along the Shipra River are sacred bathing spots. Ram Ghat is the most famous among them, especially during the Kumbh Mela which is held every 12 years.

  • Distance from Rai Guest House: 1.5 km
  • Best time to visit: Early morning or evening
  • Special Attraction: Evening aarti at Ram Ghat

Vedh Shala (Jantar Mantar)

Built by Raja Jai Singh II in the 18th century, this ancient observatory is one of the five observatories built by him. It houses various astronomical instruments.
